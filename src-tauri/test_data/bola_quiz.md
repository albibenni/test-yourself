# Broken Object Level Authorization (BOLA) Quiz

**1. By what name was BOLA historically known?**
- A) Cross-Site Request Forgery (CSRF)
- B) Insecure Direct Object Reference (IDOR)
- C) Broken Function Level Authorization (BFLA)
- D) Server-Side Request Forgery (SSRF)

**2. In the hotel analogy, what represents *authorization*?**
- A) Checking your ID at the front desk when you arrive
- B) The keycard the receptionist hands you at check-in
- C) The lock on your specific room door
- D) The room number printed on your reservation

## Answer Key & Explanations

1. **B) Insecure Direct Object Reference (IDOR)**
   - **Explanation:** The tldr states BOLA is "historically known as Insecure Direct Object Reference (IDOR)."

2. **C) The lock on your specific room door**
   - **Explanation:** The analogy maps authentication to "checking your ID at the front desk" and authorization to "the lock on your specific room door."
